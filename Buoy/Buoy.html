Types of Buoys

Navigational Buoys:

These buoys mark channels, shoals, reefs, and other navigational hazards. They provide guidance to ships and boats, indicating safe passage.

Mooring Buoys:

These buoys are used for securing vessels in place. They have a strong anchor chain or rope attached to the seafloor, allowing boats to temporarily tie up without using a dock or pier.

Enhanced Visibility:

Buoys are designed to be highly visible, even from a distance and in adverse weather conditions. They are typically painted with bright colors, equipped with reflective materials, and fitted with lights or radar reflectors.

These features improve their visibility during daytime, nighttime, and low-visibility situations, ensuring that they can be easily spotted by mariners.

Real-Time Information:

Data buoys are equipped with sensors that collect real-time environmental data. This information includes weather conditions, sea state, water temperature, salinity, wave height, and current patterns. Scientists, researchers, and meteorologists use this data to study climate patterns, monitor oceanic changes, and improve weather forecasting.

It enables mariners to make informed decisions regarding route planning, avoiding severe weather, or identifying favorable fishing grounds.
